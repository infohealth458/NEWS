UK Immigration White Paper — Health & Skilled Worker Rules

The White Paper (May 2025) introduced important rule changes implemented from 22 July 2025 — covering care workers, skilled worker thresholds, dependents, and settlement requirements.

Summary — Key Immediate Changes

  • Overseas recruitment for care workers ended: Adult social care roles (Care Worker and Senior Care Worker) are no longer open to new overseas sponsorship applications.
    (Transitional arrangements provided — see details below).
  • Skilled Worker rule change: The minimum skill level returned to degree-level (RQF Level 6); many medium/low-skilled roles are no longer eligible for sponsorship unless on a temporary shortage list.
  • Salary thresholds increased: General minimum salary thresholds and Skilled Worker salaries were updated (with higher pay requirements), taking effect around 22 July 2025.
  • Restrictions on dependents: New applicants for lower-skill roles (RQF 3–5) may no longer bring dependents.
  • Settlement (ILR) change proposal: Proposals suggest extending the residence requirement for settlement from 5 years to 10 years (with some possible exceptions).

Quick action for workers / employers: If you work in the care sector or are a sponsoring employer, you will need to review workforce planning, improve pay/conditions, and create retention strategies for existing in-UK staff.

Detailed Points

1) Care workers — End of overseas recruitment

The White Paper confirmed that overseas recruitment for care worker and senior care worker roles would close. Employers can no longer sponsor new overseas applicants in these roles. Those already in the UK are offered transitional protections (visa extensions and in-country switching for a limited duration).

2) Timing — when rules took effect

The White Paper was published in May 2025; rule changes and “Statements of Changes” instruments made them operational from 22 July 2025. Transitional protections (e.g., extensions/switching) are commonly available until 22 July 2028.

3) Skilled Worker: Skill level and salary

The Skilled Worker route now requires a minimum skill level of RQF Level 6 (degree). Previously eligible RQF 3 roles were largely removed. Salary thresholds were increased, requiring employers to offer higher pay for sponsorship. Some exemptions or temporary shortage list arrangements apply for medium-skilled roles.

4) Dependents and family

New applicants in lower-skilled roles are restricted from bringing dependents. Existing visa holders with dependents in the UK may retain protections, but the new rules apply to future applications.

5) Settlement (Indefinite Leave to Remain)

Policy proposals include extending the general qualifying period for settlement (ILR) from 5 to 10 years, with some exemptions for highly contributive workers. Both employers and migrants need to consider the long-term impact of this change.

Quick facts
  • White Paper published: May 2025 (available on GOV.UK).
  • Key implementation date: 22 July 2025 (major rules took effect).
  • Transition end (care workers): 22 July 2028 (protections end — see sponsor guidance).

Sources: GOV.UK White Paper, Home Office announcements, and legal briefings (links below).

If you are a worker

  • If already in the UK on a care role: monitor your visa expiry and switching options; stay in contact with your employer.
  • New overseas applicants: do not apply for care worker roles (route closed). Explore other eligible skilled roles (degree-level).

If you are an employer

  • Revise recruitment strategy: focus on local hiring, training, pay improvements, and retention.
  • Check Temporary Shortage List eligibility to see if medium-skilled roles remain sponsorable temporarily.
